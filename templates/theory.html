Duffing oscillator

Duffing oscillatoris an example of a periodically forced oscillator with a nonlinear elasticity, written as
x”+ δx’+ βx+ αx³= γcos wt where the damping constant obeys δ≥0 , and it is also known as a simple model which yields chaos, as well as Van Der Pol oscillator.
The Duffing oscillator is the motion of a particle in a quartic potential well V(X) = ±1/2x² + 1/4x⁴ + 1/4 giving the equation of motion (including dissipation and periodic driving) x¨ + γ x˙ ± x + x3 = g cos(ωDt)
The positive sign corresponds to an anharmonic well, and can be used to study the phenomenon of frequency pulling. With the negative sign (the “inverted” Duffing equation) the fixed point x = 0 is unstable, and there are potential minima at x = ±1

Small amplitude oscillations occur near these minima, and larger amplitude motion extends between the two minima. An experimental implementation of the Duffing equation is the “Moonbeam” consisting of a flexible beam that oscillates in the double well potential formed by the elastic restoring forces and two magnets.
